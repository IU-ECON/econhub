Arlington W. Williams Scholarship

This scholarship is named in honor of Arlie Williams, a long time member of the Economics faculty, who excelled as both researcher and teacher, while also providing the department and it students with invaluable service in his role as Director of Undergraduate Studies for almost 20 years. This award honors Arlie for the many students he taught, advised, and mentored through his distinguished career in the department. It was created through a donation from an economics alumnus who served as Professor Williams' undergraduate teaching intern. The award is given to an Economics major who has demonstrated academic excellence in their course work at IU.

This year's award winner: Jessica Ousterhout

Carroll Christenson Award

This prize goes to an outstanding junior or senior economics major who has demonstrated an ability to master the technical content of modern economics, intellectual interest of a wide scope, inclination and ability to devote skills to the study of social problems and issues that extend into the broader ranges of social science. This award is named after Carroll Christenson who was a former chair of the Economics Department and long-time faculty member.

This year's award winner: Amangul Hydyrova

Mr. & Mrs. Harold E. Strow Award

This annual award to an outstanding student with a major or minor in economics was established by the Rathmann Family Foundation to honor Mr. & Mrs. Harold Strow. Mr. Strow was their high school math teacher who received his B.A. in Economics from IU in 1924.

This year's award winner: Kemal Perdana

Graduate Student Awards

A. W. Phillip Saunders Award

The Phillip Saunders Award is given to the best introductory economics associate instructor in the department. It is named in honor of Professor Saunders who served as the faculty coordinator of our large, multi-section introductory courses from 1971 to 1998, except for the four years he served as department chair.

This award goes to the best introductory economics associate instructor in the department. In addition to the strong performance their students have demonstrated on departmental finals, their students routinely comment on the strength of their teaching abilities and the valuable experiences they have had in their classroom.

This year's award winners:

  • Guilherme Paiva Pinto
  • Cesar Salinas Depaz
Alumni Associate Instructor Award for Excellence in Teaching

This award is presented to an E370 Associate Instructor who has shown continued strong performance as an instructor of statistical labs.

Witney/LeMar Fellowship

Bruce LeMar is a 1968 graduate of Indiana University. This award was established by LeMar in honor of professor emeritus Fred Witney, whom he credits with providing the inspiration to pursue a career in labor relations. This fellowship is awarded to an outstanding graduate student in the Department of Economics with preference for students who have an interest in labor economics and/or labor relations and have a record of academic excellence.

Henry M. Oliver Award

The Oliver Award recipient for this year: To be announced

Frank T. Bachmura Award

The Frank T. Bachmura Award was established by Barbara Bachmura for an outstanding student in development economics or comparative systems. Professor Bachmura was a faculty member in economics from 1963 until his untimely death in 1975. He was known for his contributions in agricultural economics and Latin American economic development. He was concerned primarily with the problems of alleviating poverty and raising agricultural productivity. He served as a consultant for the Inter-American Development Bank, the Food and Agricultural Organization of the United Nations, the United States Agency for International Development and was a director of the American Peace Society.

This year's Bachmura Award winner: To be announced

Lloyd Orr Dissertation Fellowship

Lloyd came to IU in 1960 as a Lecturer and became Assistant Professor of Economics in 1964 upon completion of his doctorate at Northwestern University. In addition to his successful career in economic research on policy issues and how they affect incentives, including policies related to welfare incentives, Lloyd also had a keen interest in environmental issues related to sustainable economic development. His research interests had a close connection to the courses he taught, with a clear focus on public sector economics. In addition to serving as Department Chair, 1985-88, Lloyd served as Arts and Sciences Associate Dean for budget from 1970-73.

Beyond his academic career, Lloyd had many interests across a wide range of activities; playing his trombone in numerous settings including the Bloomington Community Band, flying airplanes with his close friend and colleague Scott Gordon, skiing with family, and motorcycle riding. He clearly had a heart and mind for adventure. He was also known as a strong advocate for social justice, including active involvement in various programs to assist those in need.
